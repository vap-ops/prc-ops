## 2. Who this notice covers (data subjects)

This notice covers every identifiable person whose data the application holds:

- **App users** — staff who log in (via LINE), across every role.
- **Field workers (ช่าง)** — rostered workers, whether self-claimed or
  onboarded by a crew-lead on their behalf.
- **Subcontractor crew** — including migrant workers (nationality + work-permit
  data is held).
- **Internal staff registrants** — people going through self-onboarding.
- **Contractors** — party contacts and their workers.
- **Clients** — people granted the read-only progress portal.
- **Vendor / supplier / service-provider contacts** — named contact persons.
- **Third parties named by a subject** — most notably **emergency contacts**
  (name, phone, relationship) supplied during worker/staff onboarding.

## 3. Where the data comes from

Personal data reaches the application in three ways, which matters for PDPA
notice obligations:

1. **Directly from the subject** — e.g. an app user's LINE profile at login; a
   worker or staff member self-claiming their own registration; feedback a user
   submits.
2. **From a third party on the subject's behalf ("by proxy")** — a crew-lead or
   site admin onboarding a worker who has no smartphone; a subject naming their
   emergency contact. PDPA still requires the subject be given notice; see §6.
3. **Generated by use of the app** — behavioural telemetry, audit trail, and
   read receipts created as a by-product of using the service (§4, "Activity &
   telemetry").

## 7. Your rights as a data subject (PDPA)

Subject to the Act's conditions and exemptions, you may:

- **Access** your personal data and obtain a copy (PDPA s.30).
- **Data portability** — receive/transmit data in a machine-readable form
  (s.31).
- **Object** to certain processing (s.32).
- **Erasure / destruction / anonymisation** (s.33).
- **Restrict** use of your data (s.34).
- **Rectify** inaccurate or incomplete data (s.36).
- **Withdraw consent** at any time, where processing relies on consent (s.19),
  as easily as it was given — recorded via the `revoked_at` field on the consent
  ledgers.
- **Lodge a complaint** with the Personal Data Protection Committee (PDPC).

## 11. Security measures

- **Row Level Security (RLS) is enabled on every table** — access is scoped to
  the authenticated user's role and memberships.
- **Append-only, immutable audit trail** (`audit_log`) — three-layer enforcement
  (revoked privileges, RLS, and a raise-on-mutate trigger).
- **Least-privilege database access** — the service-role key is server-only and
  never reaches the browser bundle; schema, data, Storage, and DB-role changes
  go through the reviewed migration gate
  ([`change-management.md`](./change-management.md)).
- **Secrets, not plaintext, for credentials** — invite links are stored only as
  a `token_hash`.
- **Storage** — image originals are private and never modified; watermarks are
  rendered on demand.
- **In transit** — served over HTTPS. **At rest** — Supabase-managed encryption
  (`<<FILL: confirm at-rest encryption + backup posture>>`).
