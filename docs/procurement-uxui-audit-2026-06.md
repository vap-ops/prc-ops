# Procurement UX/UI audit — confusion register (2026-06-27)

> Generated by a multi-agent audit workflow (111 agents): 6 cluster auditors + a PO-vs-items deep-dive + a terminology cross-cut, each finding adversarially verified against source, then a completeness critic. Source headline: admins cannot intuitively distinguish a PO from its PO-items.

**Totals:** 85 surfaces inventoried · 102 findings (13 confirmed / 78 partial / 11 refuted by the verifier).

Severity shown is the verifier's *corrected* severity (per-finding, atomic). NOTE: the verifier downgraded findings individually; the felt confusion is the COMPOUND of terminology+consistency drift, and several issues were independently rediscovered 6-9 times — that convergence, not the atomic score, is the priority signal. The remediation plan lives in `docs/feature-specs/211-procurement-terminology-clarity.md`.

## Completeness critic — blind spots (not in the per-cluster findings)

_The audit is broad and largely accurate — I spot-checked ~12 of the raised findings against source and the high-severity ones (สั่งซื้อแล้ว double-meaning at labels.ts:48 PR vs :126 PO; กำลังจัดส่ง triple-reuse; PO nested under /requests via order-paths.ts; the two diverging band-label sets in procurement-pipeline.ts vs request-bands.ts; the create-PO field "เลขที่ใบสั่งซื้อ / อ้างอิงผู้ขาย" at sheet line 509) all verify true. No raised finding looked wrong. However, the audit has a SURFACE-COVERAGE blind spot: it inventoried interactive screens but skipped (a) the entire LINE/push-notification text layer, (b) the global nav landmark that names the whole area, (c) several server-side helpers that ENCODE the conflation (po-detail.ts, order-stages.ts derivation, row-health, drawer-actions), and (d) cross-domain mental-model handoffs (supply-plan→PR, PR→GL voucher). The headline "PO vs PO-Items" problem is well covered; the missing pieces are the same confusion class leaking into channels the user reads OUTSIDE the screens (LINE messages, nav tabs) and the data layer that makes the labels unfixable in one place. Roughly 6 distinct gaps, 2 of them high-impact (notifications, nav landmark)._
