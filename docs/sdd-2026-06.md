# Software Design Description — prc-ops (June 2026)

**Audience and purpose.** This is the single self-contained brief for a UX/UI
agent tasked with **redesigning the prc-ops app from scratch**. It describes
everything the app does today, who uses it, the conditions it runs in, the
domain model the design must express, and the constraints that are
non-negotiable versus the surfaces that are open to reinvention. You do not
need the codebase to read this — every fact needed to redesign is stated here,
not pointed to. Where this document and a binding ADR/spec ever disagree, the
ADR/spec wins; §13 lists the things still worth a human's eye.

> **How to use this for a redesign.** Read §1–§4 to understand the product,
> the people, and the hard constraints. Read §5 for the complete list of
> screens you must account for and what each one must let users accomplish.
> Read §6 for the data and state machines the UI has to render truthfully.
> §7–§9 are the current interaction patterns, components, and visual system —
> treat them as **reference, not a cage**. §10 tells you exactly what is
> locked (cannot change) versus open (redesign freely). §11–§13 are platform
> notes, the forward roadmap to leave room for, and open questions.

---

## 1. Product context

**What it is.** A mobile-first operations app for a **Thai construction
contractor (PRC)**. It turns what used to live in chat threads, paper, and a
back-office spreadsheet into one system of record: site staff photograph work
as it progresses, project managers approve it, materials are requested and
purchased through a tracked pipeline, daily labor is captured, and PDF
progress reports are generated for clients.

**The one-sentence model.** _Field staff produce evidence (photos, labor,
purchase requests) against work packages; office staff review, decide, buy,
and report — and the work package is the center everything attaches to._

**Where it runs.** Installed as a **PWA** on field phones (standalone, no
browser chrome) and used in a normal browser on office desktops. Hosting is
Vercel; backend is Supabase (Postgres + Auth + Storage). Login is via **LINE**
(the dominant messaging app in Thailand). A LINE bot pushes notifications
(built, not yet switched on). A legacy spreadsheet system (AppSheet) still
handles some back-office writes during a planned wind-down.

**Domain in one breath:** projects → work packages → progress photos (before /
during / after) → PM approval → PDF report; plus purchasing (request → approve
→ purchase → ship → deliver), daily labor capture, and a contacts directory
(clients, suppliers, contractors, day-labor crews, service providers).

## 3. Operating constraints that shape every design decision

These are the reason the app looks the way it does. A redesign may change the
_look_ but must keep honoring the _constraint_.

### 3.1 Field-first physical reality (the SA's world)

- **Direct sunlight.** The screen is read outdoors at arm's length in glare.
  This forces a **light, high-contrast theme**, solid saturated status colors
  (no pastel translucency), thick borders, and large type. A dark theme is
  deliberately disabled.
- **Gloved hands / dirty fingers.** Tap targets are **≥44px** everywhere;
  the primary photo-capture action is a **64px full-width amber bar**. Worklist
  rows are ≥56px.
- **One-handed, on the move.** The phone surface is bottom-anchored: primary
  navigation is a bottom tab bar; the most important action sits in the
  thumb zone.
- **Flaky connectivity.** Sites have poor signal. Photo capture is
  **offline-first** (see §7.4): a photo is never lost to a dropped connection.

### 3.2 Thai-first, single language

- The entire UI is **Thai only**. There is no language switcher and no i18n
  framework. Every label, status, button, and error is Thai (full glossary in
  §9.4). The redesign must use the exact Thai terms — they are the product's
  vocabulary, not translations.
- **Buddhist-era dates, Bangkok time.** Dates render in the Thai Buddhist
  calendar (Gregorian + 543 years) and the `Asia/Bangkok` timezone, always.
  Example: 14 June 2026 → `14 มิ.ย. 2568`. Three formats are in use: date+time
  (`14 มิ.ย. 2568, 13:45`), date-only (`14 มิ.ย. 2568`), and time-only
  (`13:45`). Server and client must render identically.
- **Thai typography needs breathing room.** Thai stacks tone marks above
  characters; wrapped headings need explicit extra line-height or the marks
  collide. The font is **Sarabun** (weights 400/500/600). Codes use a mono font.

### 3.3 PWA / standalone constraints

- The app installs to the home screen and runs **without browser chrome** — so
  there is no browser back button or reload. The design provides its own
  **back affordance** (a back chip on every detail header) and a **refresh
  button** on every content page.
- **LINE login from a standalone PWA is hard** (no popup/tab model, iOS kills
  backgrounded apps). The solved flow is a device-code handoff that opens LINE
  in the same window and resumes on return. For design: the login surface must
  work as a single in-app flow, and "you are logged in, welcome back" states
  must survive the app being killed and reopened.

### 3.4 Append-only data → "you add truth, you don't erase it"

- Evidence (photos, labor logs, photo markups, purchase-request attachments)
  and the audit trail are **append-only**. There is no hard delete or edit.
  - **Editing** a record means superseding it with a new version.
  - **Removing** a photo means writing a "tombstone" (the photo disappears
    from view but the history is preserved).
- **UX consequence:** affordances are "add / replace / remove from view,"
  never "permanently delete." Corrections (e.g. fixing a labor day) are framed
  as a new corrected entry, not an in-place edit. The redesign should reflect
  this honestly — it is a system of record, not a scratchpad.

### 3.5 Money isolation

- Sensitive money fields — worker **day rates**, project **budget**, labor
  **cost/variance**, and contact **bank details** — are walled off and visible
  **only to PM/super**, never to site staff, even on shared devices.
- **UX consequence:** these fields appear only on PM/super surfaces (workers
  roster, payroll, project settings, the PM review cost view, contact detail
  bank block). The redesign must keep money out of any field-staff view and
  treat bank/rate blocks as a distinct, gated zone.

### 3.6 WP-centric information architecture

- The **work package (WP)** is the center of gravity. Every role navigates
  _to_ a work package; the project page is a list of WPs; photos, labor,
  purchase requests, and approvals all attach to a WP. There is one project
  page shared by all roles.
- **UX consequence:** the WP detail screen is the most important screen in the
  app. The WP's identity (its name) is never truncated on its own detail page.
  New capabilities should surface on the WP first, list pages second.

## 4. Information architecture & navigation

### 4.1 The navigation contract

- URLs are **content-named** (not role-prefixed): `/projects`, `/review`,
  `/requests`, `/payroll`, `/contacts`. The project page
  `/projects/[id]` is THE project page for every role.
- **Back affordance:** entering a detail screen from a hub returns to that hub
  via a back chip (not a breadcrumb trail, not a browser back).
- Two navigation layers coexist:
  - **Bottom tab bar** — phone-first, the primary nav on mobile (hidden on
    desktop). Role-specific tab sets.
  - **Hub nav strip** — desktop-only horizontal nav (hidden on phone).

### 4.2 Bottom tab sets (verified from source)

| Role                              | Tabs (in order)                                                                                         |
| --------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| `site_admin`                      | โครงการ `/projects` · คำขอซื้อ `/requests` · โปรไฟล์ `/profile`                                         |
| `project_manager` / `super_admin` | รอตรวจ `/review` · โครงการ `/projects` · คำขอซื้อ `/requests` · ติดต่อ `/contacts` · โปรไฟล์ `/profile` |
| `procurement`                     | คำขอซื้อ `/requests` · โปรไฟล์ `/profile`                                                               |

**Exception:** the **WP detail screen shows no bottom tab bar** — the photo
capture bar owns the thumb zone there. (This is a deliberate field-first
choice; the redesign should preserve "capture is king" on that screen even if
the mechanism changes.)

### 4.3 Desktop hub nav strips

- **SA:** โครงการ `/projects` · คำขอซื้อ `/requests`
- **PM/super:** รายการรอตรวจ `/review` · โครงการและรายงาน `/projects` · คำขอซื้อ `/requests` · ค่าจ้าง `/payroll` · รายชื่อติดต่อ `/contacts`

### 4.4 Known navigation seams (today's rough edges — fair game to fix)

- `/payroll` (ค่าจ้าง) is on the PM **desktop** nav only, not the phone tabs.
- `/workers` (labor roster) has **no nav entry** — reachable by URL only.
- `procurement` has no project hub and no desktop nav strip — a single
  worklist only.
- SA cannot reach the contacts directory to curate suppliers.

## 6. Domain data model & state machines

The redesign must render these truthfully — statuses, lifecycles, and the
meaning behind each color. (Tables abbreviated to what affects the UI.)

### 6.1 Core entities

- **projects** — name, code (immutable), status, client, type, address,
  budget (money-isolated), notes.
- **work_packages** — name, status, priority, description, deliverable,
  assigned contractor, planned dates, dependencies, notes. The unit of work.
- **deliverables** — named/coded groupings of work packages (used to group WPs
  on the project page and in reports).
- **photo_logs** — append-only progress photos, tagged by phase (before /
  during / after). Edits/removals via supersede/tombstone.
- **photo_markups** — append-only drawing + comment overlays on a photo (photo
  bytes themselves are never modified).
- **approvals** — append-only PM decisions on a WP (approved / rejected /
  needs_revision + comment).
- **purchase_requests** — the full requisition lifecycle in one row (see 6.3).
- **purchase_request_attachments** — append-only; reference / invoice /
  delivery-confirmation images and links.
- **labor_logs** — append-only daily labor capture per WP per worker (full or
  half day); corrections via supersede; carries rate/cost (money-isolated).
- **workers** — labor roster; own staff or day-labor (DC); day rate
  (money-isolated); may be parented to a contractor (crew).
- **contractors / suppliers / clients / service_providers** — contacts
  directory masters, each with a status (active/probation/blacklisted).
- **contact_bank** — money-isolated bank details, linked to exactly one
  contractor/supplier/service provider.
- **reports** — generated PDF jobs and their status.
- **notification_outbox** — queued LINE pushes (delivery state is mutable).
- **users** — role + LINE identity + self-editable display name.
- **audit_log** — append-only record of every significant action.

### 6.2 Statuses, their meaning, and their color (the color is semantic)

The status palette is consistent across the whole app — **learn the color once,
read it everywhere.** A redesign may change the exact hue values but should keep
the _semantic mapping_: neutral/idle, in-flight/attention, waiting/in-transit,
positive/done, negative/blocked, muted/closed.

**Work package status** — `not_started` (neutral) · `in_progress` (amber,
in-flight) · `on_hold` (amber) · `pending_approval` (amber, with PM) ·
`complete` (emerald, done).
Thai: ยังไม่เริ่ม / กำลังดำเนินการ / พักชั่วคราว / รออนุมัติ / เสร็จสิ้น.

**Project status** — `active` (neutral) · `on_hold` (amber) · `completed`
(emerald) · `archived` (muted).
Thai: กำลังดำเนินการ / พักชั่วคราว / เสร็จสิ้น / เก็บถาวร.

**Approval decision** — `approved` (emerald) · `rejected` (red) ·
`needs_revision` (amber) · none yet (neutral).
Thai: อนุมัติแล้ว / ไม่อนุมัติ / ให้แก้ไข.

**Purchase request status** — `requested` (neutral) · `approved` (emerald) ·
`rejected` (red) · `cancelled` (muted) · `purchased` (amber, ordered) ·
`on_route` (sky, in transit) · `delivered` (emerald, received) ·
`site_purchased` (emerald, on-site cash buy).
Thai: ส่งคำขอแล้ว / อนุมัติแล้ว / ไม่อนุมัติ / ยกเลิกแล้ว / สั่งซื้อแล้ว /
กำลังจัดส่ง / ได้รับของแล้ว / ซื้อหน้างาน.

**Priority (purchase request & work package)** — `normal` (neutral, no pill) ·
`urgent` (amber) · `critical` (red).
Thai: ปกติ / ด่วน / ด่วนมาก.

**Report status** — `requested` (neutral) · `processing` (amber) · `complete`
(emerald) · `failed` (red).

**Contact status** — `active` / `probation` / `blacklisted` (blacklisted is
hidden from assignment pickers).

**Photo phase** — `before` / `during` / `after` →
เตรียมงาน / ระหว่างทำ / แล้วเสร็จ.

### 6.3 Lifecycles (state machines the UI renders as steppers/progress)

**Work-package progress (photo-driven):**
`not_started` → first _during_ photo flips to `in_progress` → first _after_
photo flips to `pending_approval` → PM approves → `complete`. PM can manually
toggle `on_hold` (a during photo does **not** auto-release a hold; release
re-derives from current photos). Rejection/needs-revision sends it back with an
attention card explaining why.

**Purchase request (the 5-stage tracker):**
`requested` → PM decision: `approved` or `rejected` → back office records a
purchase (`purchased`) → records a shipment / it's on the way (`on_route`) →
delivery confirmed on site (`delivered`). After approval it may be `cancelled`.
A purchase made directly on site bypasses the pipeline as `site_purchased`.
**Status is derived from facts** (purchased-at / shipped-at / delivered-at) —
the stepper renders facts, not a hand-set status.

**Report:** `requested` → `processing` → `complete` (download link) or
`failed` (retry).

### 6.4 Contractor / labor taxonomy (matters for contacts + payroll UI)

- Contacts split into: **clients**, **suppliers**, **contractors**, **service
  providers**, and within contractors a **day-labor (DC)** category with
  subtypes (company / regular / temporary).
- **Workers** are either _own_ staff or _DC_ (day-labor), can be grouped as a
  contractor's **crew**, and carry a **day rate** that only PM/super may see.

## 7. Interaction & content patterns (how the app behaves)

These are the behaviors users have learned. Keep the behavior; the form is
yours to redesign.

### 7.1 Save lifecycle

Every save goes: button → disabled "กำลังบันทึก…" while the server round-trips →
on confirmed success a "บันทึกแล้ว" status. **Never show "saved" before the
server confirms.** Errors render inline (red strip, role="alert", ending
"กรุณาลองใหม่อีกครั้ง").

### 7.2 Optimistic updates

Low-risk toggles (e.g. WP priority) update the UI immediately and revert with a
toast on failure. Used where instant feedback matters and the write is cheap.

### 7.3 Confirmation for irreversible / weighty actions

Destructive or weighty actions (remove attachment, cancel request) go through a
themed confirm dialog (dark scrim, cancel defaults to focus). Native
`window.confirm` is never used.

### 7.4 Offline-first photo capture (the signature interaction)

A captured photo is written to device storage **immediately** (survives crash,
close, navigation, no signal). On good signal it uploads at once; on bad signal
a banner shows "waiting to send N photos" and the user can keep working or
close the app. When signal returns, a background runner drains the queue and
retries with backoff. Replays are idempotent (a re-sent photo can't double).
On a shared device, other users' queued photos are shown read-only and never
sent by the wrong person. **This must survive any redesign** — losing a site
photo is the worst failure the app can have.

### 7.5 Photo viewing & markup

Photos show as a fixed-height **horizontal filmstrip** (never a growing grid —
page height stays constant). Tap opens a lightbox with swipe/prev-next between
photos. In the lightbox a user can draw strokes and attach comments
(**markup**) as an overlay; the photo itself is never altered.

### 7.6 Notes everywhere

Most entities (work package, project, purchase request, labor day, worker)
carry a free-text **note** field — a deliberate "backup capture" so anything
the structured fields don't cover still has a home. The redesign should give
notes a consistent, easy-to-reach treatment.

### 7.7 Editing means superseding (see §3.4)

There is no in-place edit of evidence. The UI affordance for "fix this" creates
a corrected version; "remove this" hides it (tombstone) while preserving
history. Frame corrections as additive, and never offer a "delete forever"
button on evidence.

## 10. What's locked vs open for redesign

**Locked (constraints, not styling — must survive any redesign):**

1. **Thai-only UI**, exact domain vocabulary (§9.4), Buddhist-era + Bangkok
   dates.
2. **Field-first legibility:** outdoor-sun-readable, ≥44px targets, solid
   status colors readable by hue alone.
3. **Status _semantics_** (§6.2): the meaning each state carries, and the
   semantic color families. (Exact hex/OKLCH values: open.)
4. **WP-centric IA:** one shared project page; the WP detail screen is the
   focus surface; WP name never truncates on its own detail.
5. **Append-only reality:** no "delete forever" on evidence; edits are
   supersedes; removals are tombstones.
6. **Money isolation:** rates/budget/cost/bank visible to PM/super only, never
   on field-staff surfaces.
7. **Offline-first photo capture:** a captured photo is never lost; queue +
   retry + idempotent replay.
8. **PWA standalone realities:** self-provided back + refresh; login works as a
   single in-app flow that survives app death.
9. **LINE login** is the only auth.
10. **Role homes & tab/role gating** (§2, §4): who sees what.

**Open (redesign freely):**

- The entire visual language — layout, spacing, color _values_, typography
  choices, iconography, motion, component shapes.
- Navigation _mechanics_ — the tab bar vs. alternatives, how the hub strip
  works on desktop, how detail screens are entered/exited.
- The WP detail screen's composition (as long as capture stays primary).
- The purchasing stepper's visual form, the schedule/Gantt presentation, the
  contacts directory layout.
- Empty/loading/error/offline state presentation.
- Whether to introduce a design-system-level component library, dark mode
  (currently disabled — would need a decision), real logo/brand.

## 11. Platform & non-functional notes (for the agent's awareness)

- **Stack (you don't have to design around it, but it shapes the possible):**
  Next.js App Router + React + TypeScript, Tailwind v4 with the OKLCH token
  system above, Supabase backend. Server-rendered by default; interactive
  pieces are explicitly client components.
- **Accessibility today:** focus-visible rings on all interactive elements,
  aria roles on dialogs/alerts/steppers/radios, native radios under segmented
  controls, sr-only labels. Keep or raise this bar — a fuller modal focus-trap
  foundation is a known gap.
- **Performance posture:** light, server-rendered pages; horizontal photo
  strips keep page height constant; skeletons stream. Pagination on long lists
  (requests, contacts) is a known gap.
- **Responsive:** phone-first base; desktop affordances (hub strip, wider
  content max-width) appear at the `sm` breakpoint and up. One shared
  content-width token scales 2xl→7xl across breakpoints (login/profile/
  coming-soon are intentionally narrow).

## 12. Roadmap & deferred work (design with room for these)

Things known to be coming or deliberately parked. A from-scratch redesign
should leave space for them rather than be surprised later.

- **Roles not yet served** (v2/v3): project_coordinator, technician, hr,
  subcon_manager, accounting. Each will eventually need its own home surface
  and tab set. The "coming soon" holding page is their current placeholder.
- **LINE notifications** are built but **dark** — when switched on, the app may
  want a notification history/preferences surface (currently none).
- **Procurement onboarding is minimal** — a single worklist; a broader
  procurement experience (reach contacts, richer order views) is a likely
  expansion.
- **Critical-path / scheduling** has a Gantt and dependency model; a deeper
  critical-path engine and richer scheduling UI is a future direction (a
  reserved "critical" badge already exists in the system).
- **App-feel polish** (motion, optimistic UI, bottom sheets, toasts, haptics)
  is partially shipped and an explicit ongoing direction — a redesign is the
  moment to make the app feel native end-to-end.
- **Known smaller gaps:** dark-mode decision, real logo asset, list
  pagination, fuller dialog a11y, photo captions, markup-in-PDF, "my requests
  first" ordering.
- **Back-office (AppSheet) wind-down:** some procurement writes still happen in
  a legacy spreadsheet during a measured atrophy; in-app purchasing is meant to
  fully replace it. Design the in-app purchasing flows as the real home.

## 13. Open questions for the redesign (worth a human answer first)

1. **Visual direction & brand.** There is no real logo and the current look is
   utilitarian. How far should the redesign push toward a branded, polished
   feel versus staying austere for the field? Is dark mode wanted?
2. **The WP detail screen.** It carries a lot (photos, labor, requests, notes,
   assignment, schedule, decisions). Is the right answer one dense screen, a
   tabbed/sectioned screen, or progressive disclosure? This is the single
   highest-leverage screen to get right.
3. **Desktop experience.** Today desktop is "the phone, wider." PM/super do
   real desk work (review, reports, payroll, contacts, settings). Should the
   redesign give desktop a genuinely different, denser layout?
4. **Billing/cost granularity.** Money is currently per-purchase and per-labor;
   whether cost rolls up per-WP or per-deliverable (งวดงาน) shapes future cost
   surfaces.
5. **Procurement & the not-yet-served roles.** How much to design now for v2/v3
   roles versus leaving clean seams.
6. **Hold semantics.** A during-photo does not auto-release an on-hold WP;
   release re-derives from photos. Confirm this is the intended behavior the UI
   should reflect.
7. **Notifications surface.** When LINE push goes live, does the app need an
   in-app notifications/inbox view, or is LINE itself the surface?
